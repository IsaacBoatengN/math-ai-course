Visualizing Lebesgue Measure

Adjust the number of disjoint intervals on [0, 1] and see how the total length equals the Lebesgue measure of their union.

The measure of a union of disjoint sets is the sum of their individual measures. Here each interval’s length contributes directly to the total.

How to read this graphic: each blue bar is an interval inside [0, 1], and the thick line below is the number line. Because the intervals do not overlap, the union of these intervals has a measure equal to the sum of their lengths.

This concrete picture supports the key Lebesgue idea: measure is an assignment of size to sets, and for disjoint intervals it is simply their total length.
